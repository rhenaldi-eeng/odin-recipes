Rendang

Description

Rendang is a traditional Indonesian and Malaysian dish made with tender, slow-cooked beef in a rich and flavorful coconut milk and spice broth. It is a true labor of love, as the beef is simmered for hours until it is melt-in-your-mouth tender and the sauce has thickened and reduced to a thick and fragrant paste. The result is a dish that is rich, savory, and explosively flavorful, with a depth of taste that is truly unique. If you've never tried rendang before, trust us, you're in for a treat!

Ingredients

For the paste:

  • 3-4 shallots, roughly chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, thumb-sized piece
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1-4 small red chiles (such as bird chiles), minced or 1-2 tsp red chili flakes

For the rendang

  • 2 lbs beef, sirloin preferred, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 2-3 stalks lemongrass, bottom part only (not leafy parts), outer layer peeled and bashed with blunt end of knife
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp allspice
  • 2 cans coconut milk, divided
  • Oil as needed (about 2 tbsp) for browning beef
  • 4 cups cooked rice for serving
