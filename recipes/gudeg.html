Gudeg

Description

Gudeg is a delicious Indonesian dish made with tender, spicy jackfruit and served with a rich peanut sauce and a variety of flavorful garnishes. This hearty meal is perfect for anyone who loves aromatic, aromatic flavors and is sure to satisfy your appetite. Whether you're a fan of tropical fruits or just love a good comfort food, gudeg is a must-try dish that you won't be able to resist. So why wait? Try gudeg today and discover a new favorite dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 kg young jackfruit, peeled and cut into small pieces
  • 3 liters of water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 stick of ginger, bruised
  • 1 stick of galangal, bruised
  • 1 stick of kencur, bruised
  • 5 bay leaves
  • 1 stick of cinnamon
  • 1 stick of lemongrass, bruised
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 200 grams palm sugar
  • 200 ml coconut milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp oil

    • FOR THE BUMBU KACANG (PEANUT SAUCE):

  • 200 grams peanuts
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 stick of cinnamon
  • 1 stick of galangal
  • 1 stick of kencur
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 5 red chili peppers
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar

    • FOR THE PELENGKAP (GARNISHES):

  • Fried shallots
  • Emping crackers
  • Hard-boiled eggs, quartered
  • Fried tofu
  • Fried tempeh
  • Pickled green papaya
  • Sambal goreng krecek (spicy fried beef skin)

Steps

  1. Peel and cut the young jackfruit into small pieces. Place in a large pot with 3 liters of water, 1 tsp salt, 1 stick of ginger, 1 stick of galangal, 1 stick of kencur, 5 bay leaves, 1 stick of cinnamon, 1 stick of lemongrass, and 1 tsp white pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the jackfruit is tender.

  2. Add 200 grams palm sugar, 200 ml coconut milk, and 1 tsp salt to the pot. Stir well and continue cooking for another 20 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the jackfruit has absorbed the flavors of the spices.

  3. To make the bumbu kacang (peanut sauce), grind 200 grams peanuts in a food processor or with a mortar and pestle. Mix the ground peanuts with 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp white pepper, 1 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tbsp cumin seeds, 2 cloves, 1 stick of cinnamon, 1 stick of galangal, 1 stick of kencur, 5 garlic cloves, 5 red chili peppers, 2 tbsp oil, and 2 tbsp brown sugar. Place the mixture in a pan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the peanut sauce is thick and fragrant.

  4. To serve, place a portion of gudeg on a plate and top with a spoonful of peanut sauce and a selection of garnishes such as fried shallots, emping crackers, hard-boiled eggs, fried tofu, fried tempeh, pickled green papaya, and sambal goreng krecek (spicy fried beef skin). Enjoy with a side of steamed rice.
