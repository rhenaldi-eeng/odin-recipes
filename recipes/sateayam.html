Sate Ayam

Description

If you're a fan of bold, aromatic flavors, then sate ayam madura is the perfect dish for you. The marinade for the chicken is bursting with garlic, pepper, and lime juice, which infuses the meat with a tangy, savory taste. The peanut sauce is equally delicious, made with ground peanuts, spices, and sweet soy sauce that will have you licking your fingers in delight.

Steps

  1. To make the paste
    • Add the shallots, ginger, garlic, and chilis to a small blender or food processor.
    • Pulse the ingredients until they form a paste, scraping down the sides as needed. Set the paste aside.
  2. To make the rendang
    • Heat oil in a large Dutch oven or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Add the beef in batches and sear until well browned. Remove the beef and set it aside.
    • Turn the heat to medium-low and add the prepared paste to the pan.
    • Add the lemongrass, turmeric, coriander, and allspice to the pan. Cook the mixture, stirring often, until it takes on some color and appears dry. Be careful not to burn the mixture.
    • Return the beef, along with any juices, to the pan. Stir in the first can of coconut milk.
    • Cover the pan with the lid slightly ajar and adjust the heat to a very low simmer.
    • Simmer the mixture, stirring every 15 minutes or so, until the liquid is mostly evaporated and there is about an inch left in the bottom of the pan.
    • Add the remaining can of coconut milk to the pan.
    • Continue to simmer the mixture, stirring occasionally, until the beef is fork-tender and the sauce has reduced to a thick curry-like consistency. Take care not to break up the meat as you stir.
    • When the rendang is finished, the beef should be coated in a thick curry sauce. Remove the lemongrass stalks and serve the rendang with rice.
